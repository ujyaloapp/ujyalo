For students
The most common questions from Grade 10 students
Is Ujyalo really free? What's the catch?
+
No catch. Ujyalo is completely free right now — no credit card, no trial period, no hidden fees. Chapter Practice for Maths and Science is open to everyone with no signup needed.

We are in beta and focused on building the best product for Nepali students first. We will introduce optional paid features in the future, but the core experience will always be free.
✓ Free today. Free tomorrow. No surprises.
What is available right now?
+
Right now you can practice SEE Chapter Practice for Maths and Science — all chapters from the official CDC Grade 10 curriculum, with model answers explained in simple language.

Each chapter also shows you the key formulas and common mistakes students make — like having a teacher sitting next to you.

Coming soon: SEE Past Papers (2081, 2080, 2079), English, Social Studies and Nepali.
Do I need to create an account?
+
No — you can start Chapter Practice immediately without signing up. Just open ujyalo.app/chapter-practice.html and pick a chapter.

Creating a free account saves your progress so you can see which chapters you've completed and how you're improving over time. It takes less than a minute.
Will Ujyalo help me pass SEE?
+
Honest answer — Ujyalo gives you the tools. You have to do the work.

What we can say: students who regularly practice SEE-style questions and understand model answers perform better than students who only read textbooks. Answering questions is how real learning happens — not just reading.

Ujyalo is designed to make that practice easy, free, and available any time you have your phone.
Can I use Ujyalo on my phone?
+
Yes — Ujyalo is built for phones first. It works on any phone, tablet or computer. No app to download — just open ujyalo.app in your browser.

It also works on slow internet connections. We have designed it to be lightweight so students everywhere in Nepal can use it.
Are the questions from the real SEE syllabus?
+
Yes. All chapter practice questions are based on the official CDC Nepal Grade 10 curriculum — the same textbooks your school uses. We follow the exact chapter structure from the CDC Maths and Science books.

Past papers (coming soon) are the actual SEE exam papers from previous years.
Are answers in Nepali or English?
+
Answers are in simple, clear English — because the SEE exam is in English. But we explain things the way a friendly teacher would, not like a textbook.

Our goal is for a student in any part of Nepal to read a model answer and immediately understand it — without needing a tutor to explain further.

For parents
What parents want to know before trusting us with their child's education
How will I know if my child is actually studying?
+
We are building weekly parent reports — a simple email every Sunday showing exactly what your child practised, which chapters they completed, and where they need more work.

This feature is coming soon. When your child creates an account, you can add your email to receive these updates automatically.
⚙ Weekly parent reports — building now
Is my child's data safe?
+
Yes. We take privacy seriously.

• We never sell your child's data to anyone
• We never show advertisements
• Data is stored securely and used only to improve your child's learning experience

Read our full privacy policy for complete details.
Does my child need a smartphone?
+
Any phone with a browser and basic internet works — it does not need to be a new or expensive phone. Ujyalo is designed to be lightweight and fast even on older phones and slow connections.

If your child has access to a phone, tablet, or computer at school or home — they can use Ujyalo.
How is Ujyalo different from hiring a tutor?
+
A tutor is valuable — a good teacher makes a real difference. But most families in Nepal cannot afford daily tuition, and even those who can face the challenge of finding a good tutor.

Ujyalo is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, completely free. Your child can practice at 10pm before bed, or during lunch break at school. There are no appointments, no travel, no cost.

We are not replacing teachers — we are giving every student the tools to practice more, regardless of their family's situation.

Pricing & future
Honest answers about money, sustainability and what comes next
How long will Ujyalo stay free?
+
The core experience — chapter practice, past papers, model answers — will always be free. We believe every Nepali student deserves access to quality exam preparation regardless of their family's income.

In the future we may offer optional premium features for students who want more — but the free experience will always be strong and complete.
How does Ujyalo make money?
+
Right now — we don't. We are in beta and focused entirely on building something genuinely useful for students.

In the future we plan to offer optional paid features — things like AI-powered personalised feedback, prediction papers, and school dashboard plans. These will only be charged for things that genuinely deserve payment.

We will never show advertisements. We will never sell your data.
🇳🇵 Ujyalo is ad-free. Always.
Do you offer help for students who cannot afford anything at all?
+
Everything on Ujyalo right now is free — no application needed, no questions asked. A student in a remote village with basic internet access gets exactly the same experience as anyone else.

That is not a marketing line. It is the point of Ujyalo.
