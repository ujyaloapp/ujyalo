Why Ujyalo exists

The pressure is real.
The solution should be too.

Every year, hundreds of thousands of Nepali students sit for the SEE exam carrying the weight of their entire family on their shoulders.

It is not just an exam. It is pride. It is honour. It is the thing parents talk about at family gatherings and dream about quietly at night. A good result opens doors. A poor result — even after years of hard work — can quietly break hearts.

Most families do everything they can. They save money for tuition. They buy thick guidebooks. They make sacrifices. But even expensive coaching centres do not always deliver. And for the many families who cannot afford them at all? Their child faces Nepal's most important exam essentially alone.

"Where you are born should not decide how far you go."

That is the problem Ujyalo was built to solve. Not to replace teachers — but to make sure every student, rich or poor, city or village, has something in their corner. A patient, always-available, genuinely helpful study companion that knows the SEE inside out.

Free to start. Built for Nepal. Available 24 hours a day — even when no teacher is around.

Affordable matters

Coaching centres cost Rs 5,000–15,000 per month. We made Ujyalo Rs 299 because access to good education should not depend on family wealth.

Other tools are American products with Nepali stickers. Ujyalo is built from the ground up for the SEE syllabus, with Nepali context in every question.
